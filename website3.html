What is the Peregrine Falcon?

The Peregrine Falcon (Also known as Falco Peregrinus) is a species of bird found in the Falconidae family. The Peregrine is bird is more commonly noted by its speed and aerial skills, being the fastest member in the animal kingdom. The Peregrine can be found everywhere around the world accept extreme polar regions.

What does it eat?

The Peregrine Falcon only feeds on exclusively on medium-sized birds such as pigeons, doves, waterfowl, songbirds, and waders. How ever in urban areas the Peregrine's diet is made up of the Rock or Feral Pigeon, which in some cities is 80% of thier diet.

Reproduction

Reproduction of the Peregrine falcon occurs around when its 2-3 years old, when its sexually matured. During the breeding season the Peregrine is territorial. Within a breeding territory, a pair may have several nesting ledges; the number used by a pair can vary from one or two to seven in a 16 year period. The pair will have to defend their choosen nesting sites from other Peregrines and other birds like ravens and herons. When their chicks hatch (Also called egrets) they both leave for pray for their young.
